About Us

Our Founder – Dr. Salman Shakoh

Hope Kidney Foundation was founded in 1996 by Dr. Salman Shakoh, a dedicated nephrologist at Mayo Hospital, Lahore, along with his wife and two close friends. Witnessing the daily suffering of kidney failure patients who could not afford dialysis, they resolved to create a lifeline.

In 1996, they established the first free haemodialysis center at Sir Ganga Ram Hospital – a pioneering step that has since grown into a movement. Today, three centers operate in Lahore, providing thousands of free sessions annually.

We believe that in a humane society, access to life‑saving treatment should never depend on your bank balance.

Our Mission

To provide free, quality dialysis and kidney care to every low‑income patient in Pakistan, regardless of their ability to pay. We strive to save lives through direct treatment, education, and research.

Our Vision

A Pakistan where no one dies from kidney failure due to lack of funds. We envision a nation where prevention, early detection, and affordable treatment are accessible to all.

Our Journey

1996
First Center

Established the first free dialysis center at Sir Ganga Ram Hospital, Lahore, with just 2 machines – a humble beginning that saved countless lives.

2001
Expansion & Innovation

Added bicarbonate dialysis and reverse osmosis systems; expanded to 5 stations, improving treatment quality.

2014
Third Center

Opened a modern center at Gulab Devi Hospital, now our main office, equipped with the latest technology.
